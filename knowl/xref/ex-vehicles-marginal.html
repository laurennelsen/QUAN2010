(a)

What is the probability that a randomly selected vehicle is a Toyota, given it is a car?
Answer.
\begin{equation*} P(\text{Toyota}|\text{car})=\frac{P(\text{Toyota and car})}{P(\text{car})}=\frac{7/36}{26/36}=\frac{7}{26}\approx 0.269 \end{equation*}

(b)

What is the probability that a randomly selected vehicle is a truck, given it is a Ford?
Answer.
\begin{equation*} P(\text{Truck}|\text{Ford})=\frac{P(\text{Truck and Ford})}{P(\text{Ford})}=\frac{1/36}{15/36}=\frac{1}{15}\approx 0.067 \end{equation*}
